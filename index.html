About the School

A school built by the village, for its children

Paparao School — also known as Paparao Public School (Paparao Educational Academy) — is an English medium residential, co-educational private school situated at Koru Uppalapadu in the Prakasam District of Andhra Pradesh in India. The school is operational since 1986, with classes Nursery to Class 10.

It was started in 1986 by Late Mr. Radha Krishna Murthy, a native farmer of the village, and named after his brother, Late Mr. Paparao. It began as a three-class institution — LKG, UKG and Class I — with about 30 students, and added grades each year.

In 1988, the hostel facility was added and the school was converted into a residential school. It is affiliated with the Andhra Pradesh State Secondary Education Board and provides a bus facility for day boarders.

Academics

Three teaching divisions, one residential community

Administratively, the school is divided into three divisions, supported by a modern hostel. Classes run from Nursery to Class 10, following the Andhra Pradesh State Board syllabus.

Pre-Primary School

The goal of Pre Primary is to ensure that those children enrolled are ready to begin school, with activities geared towards skill and knowledge domains.
